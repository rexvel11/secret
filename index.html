To Iya

I know that when I decided to confess, I also accepted the risk of losing what we already had, but I chose to be honest anyway, because silence would’ve been a heavier kind of regret.
I understand the walls you’ve built, and the quiet distance you keep to protect your peace. You’ve been hurt before, and I would never ask you to rush your healing.
I just hope, in time, you’ll see that not everyone who reaches for you intends to hurt you. Some simply want to bring calm, not chaos. Comfort, not confusion.
If I ever get the chance to stay, even for a little while, I’d rather show you what consistency and patience feels like... not through promises, but through actions that don’t need to be explained.
I’m not asking for certainty, or anything in return... just the chance to prove that some intentions can feel safe, and that some risks are still worth taking.
I understand that you don’t want something like this right now, and that’s okay. I’m just here... not to prove anything, but to show you that care can exist without conditions.
